Color as archive: pigment choice and cultural memory in contemporary painting

Marta Keller — Berlin University of the Arts, Germany
Ama Osei — University of Ghana, Legon
RECEIVED 14.01.2026ACCEPTED 02.04.2026PUBLISHED 15.06.2026

Abstract

This article examines pigment selection as a carrier of cultural memory in contemporary painting practice. Drawing on semi-structured interviews with twenty-four artists from fourteen countries and a corpus of 180 works exhibited in international juried exhibitions between 2020 and 2025, we argue that pigment choice operates as an archival practice: a material record of provenance, migration, and inherited technique. The findings suggest implications for how juried evaluation frameworks account for material decisions.

Keywords   pigment · cultural memory · contemporary painting · juried exhibitions · materiality

1. Introduction

The materials of painting are rarely neutral. A painter's decision to grind lapis rather than reach for phthalocyanine blue, to source ochre from a family region, or to reject synthetic white carries information that exceeds the optical properties of the surface. This article treats such decisions as archival acts — deliberate inscriptions of memory, origin, and lineage into the material substrate of the work.

While scholarship on materiality in contemporary art is extensive, the specific function of pigment choice as a memory practice remains under-examined, particularly in the context of international juried exhibitions where works are evaluated at a distance from their material histories.

2. Method

The study combines two datasets: semi-structured interviews with twenty-four painters from fourteen countries, recruited through open calls on three curatorial platforms; and a corpus of 180 works shown in international juried exhibitions between 2020 and 2025, coded for declared materials and artist statements.

"The color is not chosen. It is remembered." — Interview participant 09, painter, Morocco

Interview transcripts were analyzed thematically; the corpus was examined for correlations between declared pigment provenance and thematic content, exhibition outcomes, and jury commentary where available.
