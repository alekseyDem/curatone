Color as archive: pigment choice and cultural memory in contemporary painting
Marta Keller (Berlin University of the Arts) · Ama Osei (University of Ghana)

The article examines pigment selection as a carrier of cultural memory in contemporary painting practice, drawing on interviews with twenty-four artists and a corpus of works from international juried exhibitions between 2020 and 2025.

DOI 10.63653/CTAR.2026.13 · RESEARCH ARTICLE · PP. 22–39
The juried exhibition as professional record: documentation practices in online curation
Louis Fontaine (Université Paris 8)

A survey of documentation standards across forty online curatorial platforms, assessing how certificates, archives, and published jury credentials function as verifiable professional records for artists.

DOI 10.63653/CTAR.2026.12 · REVIEW · PP. 40–52
Digital surfaces: material authenticity in post-photographic media
Sergio Andrade (NOVA University Lisbon) · Timo Virtanen (Aalto University)

A review of recent scholarship on materiality in digital and post-photographic art, with attention to how juried exhibitions evaluate authenticity in born-digital works.

Contemporary art and design as instruments of social cohesion: a cross-national study
Hanna Scholz (Curatone Research Initiative, Berlin)

Part of the journal's ongoing research initiative on the impact of contemporary art and design on global social structures; reports findings from a twelve-country comparative study.
