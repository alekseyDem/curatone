The theme

Color as subject rather than instrument. The call invites works in which color organizes meaning — as memory, as material, as system. Works are considered within their category; the jury evaluates originality, technique, composition, and interpretation of the theme.

About the competition

Full regulations →

Open Call: Colors is an international juried art competition organized by Curatone.art, an online curatorial platform based in Berlin. The call is open to artists worldwide, aged 18 and over, working in painting, drawing, and modern art. Up to three works may be submitted per artist; each work is entered in a single category and evaluated within it.

Every submission is reviewed independently by the full jury — fifteen credentialed art professionals from twelve countries — on a ten-point scale under published criteria: originality, technique, composition, and interpretation of the theme. Awards are granted in three tiers: Platinum (9.0–10 points), Gold (7.0–8.9), and Silver (5.0–6.9).

Finalists take part in the juried online exhibition and receive a numbered certificate carrying the jury's credentials, a documented exhibition record, and a permanent entry in the public winners archive. Selected winners are additionally considered for personal exhibitions and for publication on the platform and its media channels.

Curatone.art has concluded five international competitions with more than 850 submitted works from over sixteen countries, and publishes the peer-reviewed Curatone Art & Research Journal (ISSN 3054-6621) with DOI-registered articles.

Summary of results

Jury and criteria →

The open call ran from June 1 to September 6, 2026 and received 214 works from 205 artists across 16 countries. Painting was the largest category with 98 works, followed by modern art with 72 and drawing with 44. The widest representation came from Germany, the United States, the United Kingdom, France, and China.

Each work was scored independently by the full jury — fifteen art professionals from twelve countries — on a ten-point scale under the published criteria. Thirty-one works reached the award threshold: one Platinum, eight Gold, and twenty-two Silver. The average score among awarded works was 7.4.

The Platinum award was granted by unanimous decision. All finalist works are presented below and in the permanent winners archive; numbered certificates and exhibition documentation were issued to all participants on September 27, 2026.
